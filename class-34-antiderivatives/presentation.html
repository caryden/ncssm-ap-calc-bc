If differentiation goes forward, what goes backward?

Warm-Up

Find a function \( F(x) \) such that:

  1. \( F'(x) = 2x \)
  2. \( F'(x) = \cos(x) \)
  3. \( F'(x) = e^x \)

What is an Antiderivative?

Definition

A function \( F \) is an antiderivative of \( f \) on an interval \( I \) if

\( F'(x) = f(x) \) for all \( x \) in \( I \)

Example: \( F(x) = x^2 \) is an antiderivative of \( f(x) = 2x \) because \( \frac{d}{dx}[x^2] = 2x \)

The Family of Antiderivatives

Key Insight

If \( F \) is an antiderivative of \( f \), then so is \( F + C \) for any constant \( C \).

This is because \( \frac{d}{dx}[F(x) + C] = F'(x) + 0 = f(x) \)

The general antiderivative of \( f(x) \) is \( F(x) + C \)

where \( C \) is the constant of integration

A Family of Curves

All antiderivatives of \( f(x) = 2x \) are vertical shifts of each other

Indefinite Integral Notation

\( \int f(x) \, dx = F(x) + C \)

\( \int \) is the integral sign (elongated S for "sum")

\( f(x) \) is the integrand

\( dx \) indicates integration with respect to \( x \)

\( C \) is the constant of integration

Power Rule for Integration

Power Rule

\( \int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \) for \( n \neq -1 \)

Verification

\( \frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C\right] = \frac{(n+1)x^n}{n+1} = x^n \) ✓

Basic Integration Rules

\( \int k \, dx \) \( = kx + C \)
\( \int x^n \, dx \) \( = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \) (n ≠ -1)
\( \int \frac{1}{x} \, dx \) \( = \ln|x| + C \)
\( \int e^x \, dx \) \( = e^x + C \)

Trigonometric Integrals

\( \int \cos(x) \, dx \) \( = \sin(x) + C \)
\( \int \sin(x) \, dx \) \( = -\cos(x) + C \)
\( \int \sec^2(x) \, dx \) \( = \tan(x) + C \)
\( \int \csc^2(x) \, dx \) \( = -\cot(x) + C \)
\( \int \sec(x)\tan(x) \, dx \) \( = \sec(x) + C \)
\( \int \csc(x)\cot(x) \, dx \) \( = -\csc(x) + C \)

Properties of Integrals

Constant Multiple

\( \int k \cdot f(x) \, dx = k \int f(x) \, dx \)

Sum Rule

\( \int [f(x) + g(x)] \, dx = \int f(x) \, dx + \int g(x) \, dx \)

Difference Rule

\( \int [f(x) - g(x)] \, dx = \int f(x) \, dx - \int g(x) \, dx \)

Linearity

\( \int [af + bg] \, dx = a\int f \, dx + b\int g \, dx \)

Examples

1 \( \int 3x^4 \, dx = 3 \cdot \frac{x^5}{5} + C = \frac{3x^5}{5} + C \)
2 \( \int (x^2 + 2x - 5) \, dx = \frac{x^3}{3} + x^2 - 5x + C \)
3 \( \int \frac{1}{x^3} \, dx = \int x^{-3} \, dx = \frac{x^{-2}}{-2} + C = -\frac{1}{2x^2} + C \)
4 \( \int \sqrt{x} \, dx = \int x^{1/2} \, dx = \frac{x^{3/2}}{3/2} + C = \frac{2x^{3/2}}{3} + C \)

Rewrite Before Integrating

1 \( \int \frac{x^3 + 2x}{x} \, dx = \int (x^2 + 2) \, dx = \frac{x^3}{3} + 2x + C \)
2 \( \int (x + 1)^2 \, dx = \int (x^2 + 2x + 1) \, dx = \frac{x^3}{3} + x^2 + x + C \)
3 \( \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx = \int x^{-1/2} \, dx = 2x^{1/2} + C = 2\sqrt{x} + C \)

Initial Value Problems

Finding the Specific Antiderivative

Given \( f'(x) \) and a point \( (a, b) \) on the curve, we can find the specific function \( f(x) \).

Example: Find \( f(x) \) if \( f'(x) = 3x^2 \) and \( f(1) = 5 \)

1. \( f(x) = \int 3x^2 \, dx = x^3 + C \)

2. Use initial condition: \( f(1) = 1^3 + C = 5 \), so \( C = 4 \)

3. Answer: \( f(x) = x^3 + 4 \)

Selecting the Right Curve

The initial condition picks the specific curve from the family

Antiderivatives Summary

\( \int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \) for \( n \neq -1 \)

\( \int \frac{1}{x} \, dx = \ln|x| + C \)

Always add \( +C \) to indefinite integrals

Use initial conditions to find specific solutions
