Warm-Up

Find the equation of the tangent line to \( f(x) = \sqrt{x} \) at \( x = 4 \).

You'll need: \( f(4) \), \( f'(x) \), and \( f'(4) \)

Near a point, a curve looks like its tangent line

We can use the tangent line to approximate function values

Local Linear Approximation

\[ L(x) = f(a) + f'(a)(x - a) \]

This is just the tangent line equation!

\( f(x) \approx L(x) \) for \( x \) near \( a \)

Linearization of \( f(x) = \sqrt{x} \) at \( a = 4 \)

\( f(x) = \sqrt{x} \)
\( L(x) = 2 + \frac{1}{4}(x-4) \)

Example: Approximating \( \sqrt{4.1} \)

Use linearization of \( f(x) = \sqrt{x} \) at \( a = 4 \) to estimate \( \sqrt{4.1} \)

1 \( f(4) = 2 \), \( f'(x) = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} \), \( f'(4) = \frac{1}{4} \)
2 \( L(x) = 2 + \frac{1}{4}(x - 4) \)
3 \( L(4.1) = 2 + \frac{1}{4}(0.1) = 2.025 \)

Actual value: \( \sqrt{4.1} \approx 2.0248 \). Error is only 0.0002!

Approximation Accuracy

x = 4.1
Actual: 2.0248
L(x): 2.0250
Error: 0.0002

Differentials

If \( y = f(x) \), then

\[ dy = f'(x) \, dx \]

\( dy \) approximates \( \Delta y = f(x + dx) - f(x) \) for small \( dx \)

What happens when limits give us \( \frac{0}{0} \) or \( \frac{\infty}{\infty} \)?

Indeterminate Forms

These don't have obvious values!

L'Hospital's Rule

If \( \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \) gives \( \frac{0}{0} \) or \( \frac{\pm\infty}{\pm\infty} \), then:

\[ \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)} \]

(if the right side exists)

Example: Basic L'Hospital

Evaluate \( \displaystyle\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} \)

\( \frac{\sin 0}{0} = \frac{0}{0} \) \(\Rightarrow\) indeterminate!
1 Apply L'Hospital: \( \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\cos x}{1} \)
2 Evaluate: \( \frac{\cos 0}{1} = \frac{1}{1} = 1 \)

Example: Repeated Application

Evaluate \( \displaystyle\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x^2} \)

1 Check: \( \frac{1 - 1}{0} = \frac{0}{0} \) - apply L'Hospital
2 \( \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{2x} \) - still \( \frac{0}{0} \)! Apply again.
3 \( \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\cos x}{2} = \frac{1}{2} \)

Example: \( \frac{\infty}{\infty} \) Form

Evaluate \( \displaystyle\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{\ln x}{x} \)

As \( x \to \infty \): \( \frac{\infty}{\infty} \) \(\Rightarrow\) L'Hospital applies!
1 \( \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{\ln x}{x} = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{1/x}{1} = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{1}{x} = 0 \)

\( \ln x \) grows much slower than \( x \)

Why L'Hospital Works

Near \( x = 0 \): \( \sin x \approx x \), so \( \frac{\sin x}{x} \approx 1 \)

When NOT to Use L'Hospital

  • When the limit is NOT indeterminate
  • When algebraic simplification is easier
  • When differentiation creates more complexity
  • For limits that aren't quotients (directly)
  • Without checking the form first!

Today's Key Ideas

Linearization

\( L(x) = f(a) + f'(a)(x-a) \)

Approximates \( f(x) \) near \( x = a \) using the tangent line

L'Hospital's Rule

\( \lim \frac{f}{g} = \lim \frac{f'}{g'} \)

Only for \( \frac{0}{0} \) or \( \frac{\infty}{\infty} \) forms
