Given only \( f' \) and \( f'' \), can you sketch \( f \)?

The Curve Sketching Checklist

Complete Analysis

1 Domain
2 Intercepts (x and y)
3 Symmetry (even, odd, periodic)
4 Asymptotes (vertical, horizontal, oblique)
5 Increasing/Decreasing intervals (from \( f' \))
6 Local extrema
7 Concavity (from \( f'' \))
8 Inflection points

Asymptotes Review

Vertical Asymptote

\( x = a \) if \( \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = \pm\infty \)

Horizontal Asymptote

\( y = L \) if \( \lim_{x \to \pm\infty} f(x) = L \)

Oblique (Slant) Asymptote

\( y = mx + b \) if \( \lim_{x \to \pm\infty} [f(x) - (mx + b)] = 0 \)

Complete Example: \( f(x) = x^3 - 3x^2 + 2 \)

1 Domain: All real numbers. y-int: \( (0, 2) \)
2 \( f'(x) = 3x^2 - 6x = 3x(x-2) \). Critical: \( x = 0, 2 \)
3 Inc: \( (-\infty, 0) \cup (2, \infty) \). Dec: \( (0, 2) \)
4 Local max at \( (0, 2) \). Local min at \( (2, -2) \)
5 \( f''(x) = 6x - 6 \). Inflection at \( x = 1 \). CU: \( (1, \infty) \). CD: \( (-\infty, 1) \)

Sketch: \( f(x) = x^3 - 3x^2 + 2 \)

Local max: (0, 2)
Local min: (2, -2)
Inflection: (1, 0)

Example: Rational Function

Sketch \( f(x) = \frac{x^2}{x^2 - 1} \)

1 Domain: \( x \neq \pm 1 \). VA: \( x = 1, x = -1 \)
2 HA: \( y = 1 \) (degrees equal, ratio of leading coeff)
3 Intercepts: \( (0, 0) \). Symmetry: even function
4 \( f'(x) = \frac{-2x}{(x^2-1)^2} \). Critical: \( x = 0 \) (local max)

Sketch: \( f(x) = \frac{x^2}{x^2 - 1} \)

Sketching \( f \) from \( f' \)

Strategy

  1. Identify zeros of \( f' \) (potential extrema of \( f \))
  2. Note where \( f' > 0 \) (f increasing) and \( f' < 0 \) (f decreasing)
  3. Find extrema of \( f' \) (inflection points of \( f \))
  4. Where \( f' \) increasing: \( f \) concave up
  5. Start at a point and trace the curve

Key Relationships

What to Look For

  • Zeros of \( f' \) = horizontal tangents of \( f \) = potential extrema
  • Zeros of \( f'' \) = extrema of \( f' \) = potential inflection points
  • Maximum of \( f' \) = inflection point where \( f \) is steepest (increasing)
  • Minimum of \( f' \) = inflection point where \( f \) is steepest (decreasing)
