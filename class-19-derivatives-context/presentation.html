Unit 4: Contextual Applications of Differentiation

Interpreting Derivatives in Context

Making Meaning from Rates of Change

Class 19 | CED Topic 4.1 | AP Calculus BC | NCSSM

If \( f'(3) = 5 \), what does that mean?

Computation is only half the story.

Warm-Up

The temperature of coffee \( T(t) \) (in degrees Fahrenheit) after \( t \) minutes is given by:

\[ T(t) = 70 + 130e^{-0.05t} \]

  1. Find \( T'(t) \)
  2. Calculate \( T'(10) \)
  3. What does \( T'(10) \) represent in this context?

The derivative \( f'(a) \) represents the instantaneous rate of change of \( f \) with respect to its input, at \( x = a \)

The Units Framework

\[ \text{Units of } f'(x) = \frac{\text{Units of output}}{\text{Units of input}} \]

This ratio tells you exactly what the derivative measures

Rate Units in Context

The Interpretation Template

"At [input value with units], the [output quantity] is
[increasing/decreasing] at a rate of
[|derivative value|] [output units per input unit]"

AP Exam Tip

Use "increasing/decreasing" not "positive/negative" when interpreting in context

Example: Population Growth

\( P(t) \) represents the population of a town (in thousands) \( t \) years after 2020.

Given: \( P(5) = 42 \) and \( P'(5) = 1.8 \)

Interpret \( P'(5) = 1.8 \) in context.

In 2025, the population of the town is increasing at a rate of 1,800 people per year.

Visualizing the Rate

The tangent line's slope represents the instantaneous rate of change

Example: Cooling Coffee

Returning to our warm-up: \( T(t) = 70 + 130e^{-0.05t} \)

\( T'(t) = -6.5e^{-0.05t} \), so \( T'(10) \approx -3.94 \)

After 10 minutes, the temperature of the coffee is decreasing at a rate of approximately 3.94 degrees Fahrenheit per minute.

Note: We say "decreasing at a rate of 3.94" not "changing at -3.94"

Cooling Coffee: \( f'(t) < 0 \)

T'(10) = -3.94

Example: Marginal Cost

\( C(x) \) = total cost (dollars) to produce \( x \) items

Given: \( C(500) = 12,000 \) and \( C'(500) = 8.50 \)

When producing 500 items, the cost is increasing at a rate of $8.50 per item.

Economic Interpretation

\( C'(500) \approx \) the cost of producing the 501st item (marginal cost)

Estimating from Tables

\( t \) (hours) 0 2 4 6 8
\( W(t) \) (gallons) 500 420 350 295 260

Estimating \( W'(4) \)

1 Left difference: \( \frac{350 - 420}{4 - 2} = -35 \) gal/hr
2 Right difference: \( \frac{295 - 350}{6 - 4} = -27.5 \) gal/hr
3 Average (best estimate): \( \frac{-35 + (-27.5)}{2} = -31.25 \) gal/hr
At \( t = 4 \) hours, the water in the tank is decreasing at approximately 31.25 gallons per hour.

Reading Derivatives from Graphs

The slope of the tangent line at any point equals \( f'(x) \) at that point

What the Sign Tells Us

When \( f'(a) > 0 \)

The function is increasing at \( x = a \)

Output grows as input increases

When \( f'(a) < 0 \)

The function is decreasing at \( x = a \)

Output shrinks as input increases

Common Interpretation Mistakes

Incorrect

  • X "The derivative is 5"
  • X "f is positive"
  • X "The rate is negative"
  • X Missing units

Correct

  • + Include the input value
  • + Use "increasing/decreasing"
  • + State "rate of |value|"
  • + Include units
