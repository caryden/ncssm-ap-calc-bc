Unit 6: Integration

Integration by Partial Fractions

Class 37 | CED Topic 6.12

Breaking Complex Fractions into Simpler Pieces

Warm-Up

Verify that:

\( \frac{1}{x-1} + \frac{1}{x+1} = \frac{2x}{x^2-1} \)

Which form is easier to integrate?

The Big Idea

Complex rational functions can be decomposed
into sums of simpler fractions

↓

Each simpler fraction is easy to integrate!

\( \int \frac{2x}{x^2-1} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{x-1} \, dx + \int \frac{1}{x+1} \, dx \)

When to Use Partial Fractions

Use partial fractions when:

  • You have a rational function \( \frac{P(x)}{Q(x)} \)
  • The degree of \(P\) is less than the degree of \(Q\)
  • The denominator can be factored

Note: If deg(\(P\)) ≥ deg(\(Q\)), do polynomial division first!

Case 1: Distinct Linear Factors

When \( Q(x) = (x - a)(x - b)(x - c) \ldots \)

\( \frac{P(x)}{(x-a)(x-b)} = \frac{A}{x-a} + \frac{B}{x-b} \)

Example:

\( \frac{3x+5}{(x+1)(x-2)} = \frac{A}{x+1} + \frac{B}{x-2} \)

Finding A and B

\( \frac{3x+5}{(x+1)(x-2)} = \frac{A}{x+1} + \frac{B}{x-2} \)

Multiply both sides by \( (x+1)(x-2) \):

\( 3x + 5 = A(x-2) + B(x+1) \)

Method 1: Plug in strategic values:
\( x = 2 \): \( 11 = 3B \) → \( B = \frac{11}{3} \)
\( x = -1 \): \( 2 = -3A \) → \( A = -\frac{2}{3} \)

Completing Example 1

\( \int \frac{3x+5}{(x+1)(x-2)} \, dx = \int \left( \frac{-2/3}{x+1} + \frac{11/3}{x-2} \right) dx \)
\( = -\frac{2}{3}\ln|x+1| + \frac{11}{3}\ln|x-2| + C \)

Case 2: Repeated Linear Factors

When \( Q(x) \) has a factor \( (x - a)^n \)

\( \frac{P(x)}{(x-a)^3} = \frac{A}{x-a} + \frac{B}{(x-a)^2} + \frac{C}{(x-a)^3} \)

Example:

\( \frac{x+2}{(x-1)^2} = \frac{A}{x-1} + \frac{B}{(x-1)^2} \)

Solving Case 2 Example

\( \frac{x+2}{(x-1)^2} = \frac{A}{x-1} + \frac{B}{(x-1)^2} \)

Multiply by \( (x-1)^2 \):

\( x + 2 = A(x-1) + B \)

\( x = 1 \): \( 3 = B \)
Compare coefficients of \(x\): \( 1 = A \)

\( \int \frac{x+2}{(x-1)^2} \, dx = \ln|x-1| - \frac{3}{x-1} + C \)

Case 3: Irreducible Quadratic Factors

When \( Q(x) \) has an irreducible quadratic \( ax^2 + bx + c \)

\( \frac{P(x)}{(x-a)(x^2+1)} = \frac{A}{x-a} + \frac{Bx+C}{x^2+1} \)

Note: The numerator of the quadratic term has degree 1

Case 3 Example

\( \displaystyle\int \frac{2x^2 - x + 4}{x^3 + 4x} \, dx \)

Factor: \( x^3 + 4x = x(x^2 + 4) \)

\( \frac{2x^2 - x + 4}{x(x^2+4)} = \frac{A}{x} + \frac{Bx+C}{x^2+4} \)

Solving: \( A = 1 \), \( B = 1 \), \( C = -1 \)

Integrating Case 3

\( \int \left( \frac{1}{x} + \frac{x-1}{x^2+4} \right) dx \)

Split the second term:

\( = \int \frac{1}{x} \, dx + \int \frac{x}{x^2+4} \, dx - \int \frac{1}{x^2+4} \, dx \)
\( = \ln|x| + \frac{1}{2}\ln(x^2+4) - \frac{1}{2}\arctan\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) + C \)

Partial Fractions Summary

Distinct Linear

\( \frac{1}{(x-a)(x-b)} \)

→ \( \frac{A}{x-a} + \frac{B}{x-b} \)

Repeated Linear

\( \frac{1}{(x-a)^2} \)

→ \( \frac{A}{x-a} + \frac{B}{(x-a)^2} \)

Irreducible Quadratic

\( \frac{1}{x(x^2+1)} \) → \( \frac{A}{x} + \frac{Bx+C}{x^2+1} \)

Useful Integration Formulas

\( \int \frac{1}{x-a} \, dx = \ln|x-a| + C \)
\( \int \frac{1}{(x-a)^n} \, dx = \frac{-1}{(n-1)(x-a)^{n-1}} + C \) for \(n \neq 1\)
\( \int \frac{1}{x^2+a^2} \, dx = \frac{1}{a}\arctan\left(\frac{x}{a}\right) + C \)
\( \int \frac{x}{x^2+a^2} \, dx = \frac{1}{2}\ln(x^2+a^2) + C \)

Practice Problem 1

Evaluate: \( \displaystyle\int \frac{5x - 3}{x^2 - 2x - 3} \, dx \)

Hint: Factor the denominator first!

\( x^2 - 2x - 3 = (x-3)(x+1) \)

Practice Problem 2

Evaluate: \( \displaystyle\int \frac{3x + 1}{(x+1)^2} \, dx \)

This is a repeated linear factor case.

Practice Problem 3

Evaluate: \( \displaystyle\int \frac{1}{x^3 - x} \, dx \)

Hint: \( x^3 - x = x(x-1)(x+1) \)

Exit Ticket

1. What is the partial fraction form of \( \frac{1}{x(x+2)} \)?

2. What is the partial fraction form of \( \frac{x}{(x-1)(x^2+1)} \)?

3. Evaluate \( \displaystyle\int \frac{2}{x^2-1} \, dx \)

Key Takeaways

• Partial fractions decomposes complex fractions into simpler ones

• Three main cases: distinct linear, repeated linear, irreducible quadratic

• Factor the denominator first

• Find coefficients by strategic substitution or comparing coefficients

• Result: sums of \( \ln \), powers, and \( \arctan \)
