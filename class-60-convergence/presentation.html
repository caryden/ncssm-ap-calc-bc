Absolute & Conditional Convergence

Class 60 | CED Topic 10.9

Understanding How Series Converge

Warm-Up

We know \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n} \) converges (by AST).

But what about \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left| \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n} \right| = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n} \)?

The second series diverges! Does this matter?

Absolute Convergence

A series \( \sum a_n \) converges absolutely if:

\( \sum |a_n| \) converges

The series of absolute values must converge.

Conditional Convergence

A series \( \sum a_n \) converges conditionally if:

1. \( \sum a_n \) converges, BUT

2. \( \sum |a_n| \) diverges

Example: Alternating harmonic series \( \sum \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n} \)

Absolute Convergence Theorem

If \( \sum |a_n| \) converges, then \( \sum a_n \) converges.

Absolute convergence implies convergence!

The converse is NOT true (conditional convergence exists).

Classifying Series

Every series falls into one category:

Absolutely Convergent

\( \sum a_n \) converges
\( \sum |a_n| \) converges

Conditionally Convergent

\( \sum a_n \) converges
\( \sum |a_n| \) diverges

Divergent

\( \sum a_n \) diverges

Example 1

Classify: \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n}{n^2} \)

Step 1: Check \( \sum |a_n| = \sum \frac{1}{n^2} \)

This is a p-series with \( p = 2 > 1 \) → converges

Since \( \sum |a_n| \) converges: Absolutely convergent

Example 2

Classify: \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n}{\sqrt{n}} \)

Step 1: Check \( \sum |a_n| = \sum \frac{1}{\sqrt{n}} \)

p-series with \( p = \frac{1}{2} < 1 \) → diverges

Step 2: Check \( \sum a_n \) using AST → converges

Conditionally convergent

Testing Strategy

Start: Given \( \sum a_n \)
↓
Test \( \sum |a_n| \) — does it converge?
↓
Yes → Absolutely convergent
No → Test \( \sum a_n \) directly (AST, etc.)

Series Convergence Toolkit

For \( \sum |a_n| \):

• p-series test

• Geometric series test

• Ratio/Root test

• Comparison tests

For \( \sum a_n \):

• Alternating Series Test

• Divergence Test

Why Does This Matter?

Absolutely convergent series:

• Can be rearranged without changing the sum

• Better behaved for manipulation

Conditionally convergent series:

• Rearrangement can change the sum!

• Can even make it sum to any value (Riemann series theorem)

Exit Ticket

3. Give an example of a conditionally convergent series.

Key Takeaways

• Absolute convergence: \( \sum |a_n| \) converges

• Conditional convergence: \( \sum a_n \) converges but \( \sum |a_n| \) diverges

• Absolute convergence → convergence (but not vice versa)

• Test \( \sum |a_n| \) first, then test \( \sum a_n \) if needed

• Absolute convergence is "stronger" and better behaved
