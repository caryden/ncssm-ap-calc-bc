Finding Volumes of Revolution

Warm-Up

What is the area of a circle with radius \( r \)?

\( A = \pi r^2 \)

What about the area of a "washer" (ring) with outer radius \( R \) and inner radius \( r \)?

Area of a Washer

\( A = \pi R^2 - \pi r^2 = \pi(R^2 - r^2) \)

Outer circle minus inner circle

Solids of Revolution

When a region is rotated around an axis, it creates a 3D solid.

Common Examples

• Cone
• Sphere
• Torus (donut)
• Vase shapes

Key Question

How do we find the volume of these solids?

The Disc Method Idea

Slice the solid into thin circular discs

Each disc has:
• Radius = \( f(x) \) (or \( g(y) \))
• Thickness = \( dx \) (or \( dy \))

Volume of one disc: \( dV = \pi [f(x)]^2 \, dx \)

Disc Method Formula

Rotating around the x-axis:

\( V = \int_a^b \pi [f(x)]^2 \, dx \)

The radius of each disc is the function value \( f(x) \)

Visualizing the Disc Method

Cross-section perpendicular to axis of rotation

Example: Volume of a Cone

Rotate \( y = \frac{r}{h}x \) from \( x = 0 \) to \( x = h \) around the x-axis.

\( V = \int_0^h \pi \left(\frac{r}{h}x\right)^2 dx = \pi \frac{r^2}{h^2} \int_0^h x^2 \, dx \)
\( = \pi \frac{r^2}{h^2} \cdot \frac{h^3}{3} = \frac{\pi r^2 h}{3} \)

✓ This matches the known formula for cone volume!

The Washer Method

When there's a gap between the region and the axis of rotation, each cross-section is a washer, not a disc.

• Outer radius: \( R(x) \)
• Inner radius: \( r(x) \)

Washer Method Formula

\( V = \int_a^b \pi \left([R(x)]^2 - [r(x)]^2\right) dx \)

Outer radius squared minus inner radius squared

Visualizing the Washer Method

Region between two curves rotated around x-axis

Example: Washer Method

Find the volume when the region between \( y = x^2 \) and \( y = x \) (from \( x = 0 \) to \( x = 1 \)) is rotated around the x-axis.

Outer: \( R = x \), Inner: \( r = x^2 \)

\( V = \int_0^1 \pi (x^2 - x^4) \, dx = \pi \left[\frac{x^3}{3} - \frac{x^5}{5}\right]_0^1 \)
\( = \pi \left(\frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{5}\right) = \frac{2\pi}{15} \)

Rotation Around the y-axis

When rotating around the y-axis, solve for \( x \) in terms of \( y \):

\( V = \int_c^d \pi [g(y)]^2 \, dy \)

Or use the washer method:

\( V = \int_c^d \pi ([R(y)]^2 - [r(y)]^2) \, dy \)

Rotation Around Other Lines

For rotation around \( y = k \) or \( x = k \):

Key: Radius = distance from curve to axis

Around \( y = k \): radius = \( |f(x) - k| \)

Around \( x = k \): radius = \( |g(y) - k| \)

Always draw a picture to determine outer and inner radii!

Steps for Setting Up Volume Integrals

  1. Sketch the region and axis of rotation
  2. Determine if slicing perpendicular to axis gives discs or washers
  3. Identify outer radius \( R \) and inner radius \( r \)
  4. Set up the integral with appropriate bounds
  5. Evaluate

Practice Problem 1

Find the volume when the region bounded by \( y = \sqrt{x} \), \( y = 0 \), and \( x = 4 \) is rotated around the x-axis.

Hint: This is a disc method problem!

Practice Problem 2

Hint: Integrate with respect to \( y \)

Practice Problem 3

Find the volume when the region bounded by \( y = x \) and \( y = x^2 \) is rotated around the line \( y = 2 \).

Hint: Both radii are measured from \( y = 2 \)

Key Takeaways

• Disc Method: \( V = \pi \int [f(x)]^2 \, dx \) (solid region to axis)

• Washer Method: \( V = \pi \int (R^2 - r^2) \, dx \) (gap between region and axis)

• Radius = distance from curve to axis of rotation

• Always draw a picture to identify radii!

• Can rotate around x-axis, y-axis, or other lines
