The Mean Value Theorem

Connecting Average and Instantaneous Rates

Class 24 | CED Topic 5.1 | AP Calculus BC | NCSSM

If your average speed was 60 mph, must there be a moment when your speed was exactly 60 mph?

Warm-Up

Let \( f(x) = x^2 \) on the interval \( [1, 4] \).

  1. Find the average rate of change of \( f \) on \( [1, 4] \)
  2. Find \( f'(x) \)
  3. Is there a value \( c \) in \( (1, 4) \) where \( f'(c) \) equals the average rate?

Rolle's Theorem (A Special Case)

If \( f \) is continuous on \( [a, b] \), differentiable on \( (a, b) \), and \( f(a) = f(b) \), then

\[ \text{there exists } c \in (a, b) \text{ such that } f'(c) = 0 \]

If you start and end at the same height, there must be a horizontal tangent somewhere!

Visualizing Rolle's Theorem

The horizontal tangent must exist when \( f(a) = f(b) \)

The Mean Value Theorem

Mean Value Theorem

Hypotheses

1. \( f \) is continuous on \( [a, b] \)

2. \( f \) is differentiable on \( (a, b) \)

Conclusion: There exists \( c \in (a, b) \) such that:

\[ f'(c) = \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a} \]

The tangent line is parallel to the secant line at some point

Slope of tangent = Slope of secant (at least once)

The Speeding Ticket Interpretation

Real-World Application

If you drive 120 miles in 2 hours:

Average speed = 60 mph

By MVT, at some moment your instantaneous speed was exactly 60 mph.

This is why toll road cameras at two points can prove speeding!

Example: Finding c

For \( f(x) = x^3 - x \) on \( [-1, 2] \), find all values of \( c \) guaranteed by MVT.

1 \( f(-1) = -1 + 1 = 0 \), \( f(2) = 8 - 2 = 6 \)
2 Average rate: \( \frac{6 - 0}{2 - (-1)} = \frac{6}{3} = 2 \)
3 \( f'(x) = 3x^2 - 1 \). Set \( 3x^2 - 1 = 2 \)
4 \( x^2 = 1 \implies x = \pm 1 \). Only \( c = 1 \) is in \( (-1, 2) \)

Exploring MVT: \( f(x) = x^3 - x \)

c = 1.00
Secant slope: 2.00
f'(c) = 2.00

When MVT Doesn't Apply

Consider \( f(x) = |x| \) on \( [-1, 1] \).

1 \( f(-1) = 1 \), \( f(1) = 1 \). Average rate = 0.
2 Is there \( c \) where \( f'(c) = 0 \)?
3 \( f'(x) = -1 \) for \( x < 0 \), \( f'(x) = 1 \) for \( x > 0 \)
4 No! \( f \) is not differentiable at \( x = 0 \)

Important Consequences of MVT

  • If \( f'(x) = 0 \) for all \( x \) in an interval, then \( f \) is constant
  • If \( f'(x) > 0 \) for all \( x \), then \( f \) is increasing
  • If \( f'(x) < 0 \) for all \( x \), then \( f \) is decreasing
  • If \( f'(x) = g'(x) \) for all \( x \), then \( f(x) = g(x) + C \)

Why \( f' = 0 \) implies \( f \) is constant

Proof sketch using MVT

Take any two points \( x_1 < x_2 \) in the interval.

By MVT, there exists \( c \) between them with:

\( f(x_2) - f(x_1) = f'(c)(x_2 - x_1) = 0 \cdot (x_2 - x_1) = 0 \)

Therefore \( f(x_2) = f(x_1) \) for all pairs!

Application: Bounding Function Values

If \( f(2) = 5 \) and \( |f'(x)| \leq 3 \) for all \( x \), what bounds \( f(6) \)?

1 By MVT: \( \frac{f(6) - f(2)}{6 - 2} = f'(c) \) for some \( c \)
2 \( f(6) - 5 = 4f'(c) \), and \( |f'(c)| \leq 3 \)
3 \( |f(6) - 5| \leq 4 \cdot 3 = 12 \)
4 Therefore \( -7 \leq f(6) \leq 17 \)

Using MVT for Existence

Prove that \( f(x) = x^3 + x - 1 \) has exactly one real root.

1 \( f(0) = -1 < 0 \) and \( f(1) = 1 > 0 \), so a root exists by IVT
2 \( f'(x) = 3x^2 + 1 > 0 \) for all \( x \)
3 By MVT consequence, \( f \) is strictly increasing
4 An increasing function can cross zero at most once

Mean Value Theorem: Summary

If \( f \) is continuous on \( [a,b] \) and differentiable on \( (a,b) \):

\[ f'(c) = \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a} \quad \text{for some } c \in (a, b) \]

Geometric: Tangent parallel to secant

Physical: Instantaneous rate = average rate at some moment
