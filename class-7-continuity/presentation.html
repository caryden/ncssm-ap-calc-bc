Unit 1: Limits and Continuity

Continuity

Functions Without Breaks

Class 7 | February 13, 2026 | CED 1.10, 1.11

What Does "Continuous" Mean?

Informal: "Draw without lifting your pencil"

Intuition is good. Definitions are better.

Let's make "continuous" mathematically precise.

Continuity at a Point

A function f is continuous at x = a if and only if:

1 \(f(a)\) is defined
2 \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to a} f(x)\) exists
3 \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a)\)

All three must hold!

Condition 1: f(a) is defined

The function must have a value at the point

Condition 2: Limit exists

Left and right must approach the same value

Condition 3: Limit = f(a)

Where it's heading must be where it is

Continuity: All Three

Definition of Continuity

f is continuous at a if: f(a) exists, \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x)\) exists, and they are equal.

When continuity fails, we have a discontinuity.

Different failures create different types.

Removable Discontinuity

+ Limit exists

- f(a) undefined or ≠ limit

! Can be "fixed" by redefining f(a)

Example: Removable

\[f(x) = \frac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1} \text{ at } x = 1\]
1
f(1) = \(\frac{0}{0}\) — undefined
2
Limit exists: \(\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{(x-1)(x+1)}{x-1} = 2\)
!
Fix: Define f(1) = 2 to make it continuous

Jump Discontinuity

- Left limit ≠ Right limit

- Limit does not exist

! Cannot be fixed

Infinite Discontinuity

- Function approaches ±∞

- Vertical asymptote

! Cannot be fixed

Types of Discontinuities

Removable
Limit exists but ≠ f(a)
Fixable
Jump
Left ≠ Right
Not fixable
Infinite
Goes to ±∞
Not fixable

Continuity on Intervals

Open interval (a, b):

f is continuous at every point in (a, b)

Closed interval [a, b]:

Continuous on (a, b), plus:

  • \(\lim_{x \to a^+} f(x) = f(a)\) (right-continuous at a)
  • \(\lim_{x \to b^-} f(x) = f(b)\) (left-continuous at b)

Functions Continuous on Their Domains

+ Polynomials
+ Rational functions*
+ Trigonometric functions
+ Exponential functions
+ Logarithmic functions*
+ Root functions*

*Where defined (i.e., on their domains)

Example: Finding Intervals

Where is \(f(x) = \frac{x + 2}{x^2 - 4}\) continuous?
1
Factor: \(x^2 - 4 = (x-2)(x+2)\)
2
Undefined at x = 2 and x = -2
✓
Continuous on: \((-\infty, -2) \cup (-2, 2) \cup (2, \infty)\)

Your Turn

Find the intervals of continuity for:

\[g(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x - 1}}\]

Answer: \((1, \infty)\)

Limits of Compositions

Composition Theorem

If f is continuous at b and \(\lim_{x \to a} g(x) = b\), then:

\[\lim_{x \to a} f(g(x)) = f\left(\lim_{x \to a} g(x)\right) = f(b)\]

We can "pass the limit inside" a continuous function!

Example: Using Continuity

\[\lim_{x \to 2} \sin(x^2 - 4)\]
1
sin is continuous everywhere
2
Pass limit inside: \(\sin\left(\lim_{x \to 2}(x^2 - 4)\right)\)
3
Evaluate inner limit: \(\sin(4 - 4) = \sin(0)\)
✓
= 0

Example 2

\[\lim_{x \to 0} e^{x + 1}\]
1
\(e^x\) is continuous everywhere
2
Pass limit inside: \(e^{\lim_{x \to 0}(x+1)}\)
✓
\(e^1 = e\)

Continuity Checklist

Condition Check If Fails...
1. f(a) defined Evaluate f(a) Removable
2. Limit exists Check left = right Jump or Infinite
3. Limit = f(a) Compare values Removable

Key Takeaways

  • Continuity requires: defined, limit exists, limit = value
  • Three types: removable, jump, infinite
  • Continuous functions let us pass limits inside
  • Most "nice" functions are continuous on their domains

Exit Ticket

For what value of k is f continuous at x = 2?

\[f(x) = \begin{cases} x^2 - 1 & x < 2 \\ k & x \geq 2 \end{cases}\]
